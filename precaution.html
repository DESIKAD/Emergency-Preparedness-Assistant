Emergency Preparedness Precautions

Before an Earthquake:

  • Secure heavy furniture: Anchor bookshelves, cabinets, and other heavy furniture to the walls.
  • Check structural integrity: Ensure your home is built to withstand earthquakes.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Stock essentials like water, food, first-aid supplies, flashlight, and medications.
  • Create a family emergency plan: Establish meeting points and emergency contact details with your family members.
  • Know how to turn off utilities: Learn how to turn off gas, electricity, and water in your home.

During an Earthquake:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay indoors: Avoid running outside during the earthquake; stay away from windows and glass.
  • If driving, stop safely: Pull over to a safe spot and remain in the vehicle until the shaking stops.
  • Stay away from hazards: Move away from buildings, trees, and power lines if you're outdoors.

After an Earthquake:

  • Check for injuries: Administer first aid and call emergency services if necessary.
  • Inspect the environment: Look for hazards like gas leaks, fires, or structural damage.
  • Stay informed: Use a battery-powered radio or smartphone to stay updated on aftershocks and emergency notifications.

Before a Tsunami:

  • Stay informed: Listen for tsunami alerts or warnings on the radio, television, or through emergency services.
  • Identify evacuation routes: Know the safe evacuation routes to higher ground in case of a tsunami warning.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Ensure your emergency kit includes necessary items such as water, food, and first aid supplies.
  • Move to higher ground: If you are near the coast, evacuate immediately to higher ground when a tsunami warning is issued.

During a Tsunami:

  • Follow evacuation orders: Immediately move to higher ground and away from water.
  • Do not wait: Tsunamis can strike within minutes after an earthquake, so don't wait for official alerts.
  • Stay away from beaches: Avoid the coastline until authorities confirm it is safe to return.

Before a Fire Accident:

  • Install smoke detectors: Make sure smoke detectors are installed and tested regularly.
  • Have a fire extinguisher: Keep a fire extinguisher in your kitchen, garage, and near high-risk areas.
  • Know fire escape routes: Establish escape routes and meeting points for your family in case of fire.
  • Store flammable materials properly: Keep flammable materials away from heat sources.

During a Fire Accident:

  • Get out of the building: Evacuate the building immediately and use the closest exit.
  • Stop, drop, and roll: If your clothes catch fire, stop, drop to the ground, and roll to put out the flames.
  • Stay low: Smoke rises, so stay low to the ground where the air is clearer.
  • Use the phone carefully: Call emergency services, but only if it is safe to do so.

Before a Flood:

  • Know your flood risk: Check if your area is prone to flooding and keep informed about flood alerts.
  • Elevate electrical systems: Keep electrical panels, sockets, and other appliances above potential flood levels.
  • Keep sandbags ready: Use sandbags to protect doors, windows, and other vulnerable areas.
  • Have a flood emergency kit: Include essentials like water, food, medicines, and documents in a waterproof container.

During a Flood:

  • Move to higher ground: Avoid walking, driving, or swimming in floodwaters. Seek higher ground immediately.
  • Stay away from electrical equipment: Do not touch electrical appliances or power lines in flooded areas.
  • Evacuate when ordered: If authorities issue evacuation orders, follow them immediately.

Before a Landslide:

  • Monitor weather reports: Stay informed of heavy rainfall or snowmelt that could trigger a landslide.
  • Build on stable ground: If you live in a hilly area, ensure your home is built on stable, well-drained ground.
  • Install retaining walls: Use barriers to reduce the risk of landslides on your property.

During a Landslide:

  • Get to higher ground: Move to higher ground away from the landslide path.
  • Avoid river valleys: Stay away from river valleys, as they may be more prone to landslides during heavy rain.

Before a Tornado:

  • Know your risk: Understand the tornado risk in your area and have a plan in place.
  • Prepare an emergency kit: Include essentials like food, water, medications, and a flashlight.
  • Secure outdoor objects: Bring in or tie down outdoor furniture or objects that could become dangerous in strong winds.

During a Tornado:

  • Take shelter immediately: Head to a basement, storm cellar, or an interior room without windows.
  • Avoid large open spaces: Stay away from halls, gymnasiums, and auditoriums.
  • Protect your head: Use a mattress, heavy blankets, or cushions to protect your head and neck.

After a Tornado:

  • Wait for an all-clear signal: Only leave your shelter when authorities give the all-clear signal.
  • Inspect your surroundings: Check for injuries, structural damage, and hazards like gas leaks or downed power lines.
  • Stay away from debris: Avoid areas with fallen trees, wires, and debris until emergency services arrive.
